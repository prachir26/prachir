1.Introduction¶

The Background: Freedom Debt Relief’s customers are people who have a substantial amount of debt and because of an unexpected hardship, are no longer able to make their minimum monthly payments. Upon enrolling in Freedom’s Debt Relief program, customers cease making payments to their creditors and instead make deposits they can afford into a new dedicated bank account with Freedom. Freedom uses these funds to negotiate with each of the client’s creditors to settle the debt, typically at only a fraction of what was originally owed. Once a settlement has been agreed upon for an individual account, Freedom facilitates payments from the dedicated bank account to the creditor based on the terms of the settlement agreement. Freedom then collects fees from the client for the individual account that was settled. Freedom earns fees for each account it successfully negotiates a settlement agreement. The number of settlement agreements Freedom can negotiate is proportional to the monthly deposited amount. Assume industry statistics for any analysis input that you would want to use that is not provided here, including but not limited to fee percentage Freedom would collect along with ongoing cost per client between initial enrollment and program graduation.

The Question: Imagine that Freedom ran a recent marketing campaign to promote the value proposition of how the debt relief program helps people achieve financial freedom. Assume the cost of this campaign was $5 million. There are five months of data in the datasets provided. Let’s say campaign took place over the course of the third month. You now want to show the marketing, sales and operations teams just how successful this campaign was. Using the three datasets given:

  1. Provide a quantitative assessment of whether the marketing campaign was successful. How and why did you choose your specific success metric(s)?
  2. Based on the provided data, how would you recommend campaign strategy be adjusted in the future to improve performance?
  3. How to do you think campaign performance would have changed if we did not run the campaign in Month 3, but instead postponed it until month 6? Provide an incremental number versus your result in Question #1.
  4. Is there anything not in the provided dataset that you think would provide value if added? If so, how would you strategize gathering and leveraging the additional information?

3.2.5 Statistical Significance p Value Test¶

From Our above Exploratory data analysis, it is clear that Campaign was successful and we observed below three factors:-

  1. Number of customers and Deposits are increased during the Campaign.
  2. Sum of deposit amounts got increassed during the Campaign.
  3. Similar trend were continue post Campaign as well.

But Still before reaching to conclusion we need to perform a statistical test to prove our analysis are correct and there are no random errors.

To prove this we will perform the P Value Teast, we will take 2 groups Control Group and Test Group

Control Group Sample - Month 1 Pre-Campaign data

Test Group Sample - Month 3 Campaign data

In [41]:
rvs1 = df_month_1['deposit_amount']
rvs2 = df_month_3['deposit_amount']
stats.ttest_ind(rvs1, rvs2)
Out[41]:
Ttest_indResult(statistic=2.507908464810577, pvalue=0.012146564326727331)

As we have observed above that p Value is less than 0.05 so we can reject the null hypothesis and consider this as a successful campaign.¶
